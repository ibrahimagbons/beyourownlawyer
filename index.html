BE YOUR OWN LAWYER

Your go to source to understanding legal concept and self representation


Law is a system of rules and guidelines, usually enforced through a set of institutions, created and enforced by social or governmental institutions to regulate behavior. Law serves as a mediator of relations between people and shapes politics, economics, and society in numerous ways. According to Sir John Salmond, law is “the body of principles recognized and applied by the state in the administration of justice.”...

Legal and equitable remedies play crucial roles in addressing breaches of contract and ensuring justice is served. Damages, specific performance, and injunctions are primary tools for enforcing contractual obligations and compensating for losses. Quantum meruit claims and quasi contracts ensure fairness in situations where formal agreements are absent or incomplete. Understanding these remedies, supported by relevant Nigerian cases and sections, is essential for navigating contract disputes effectively...

The law of torts in Nigeria is a complex and evolving field that balances the need to compensate victims of wrongs with the necessity of not unduly burdening defendants. Rooted in English common law, it has been adapted to meet the specific needs and circumstances of Nigerian society. Understanding the principles of liability, the reception of tort law in Nigeria, and the available defenses and remedies is crucial for navigating this area of law effectively...

Understanding the meaning, nature, sources, and essential elements of commercial law and the sale of goods in Nigeria is crucial for ensuring compliance and protecting the interests of all parties involved in commercial transactions. The legal framework provided by statutes like the Sale of Goods Act 1893 and judicial precedents ensures that commercial activities are conducted fairly and efficiently...

Promoters play a vital role in the formation of a company, and their duties are clearly defined under Nigerian law. By adhering to their fiduciary duties, exercising care and skill, and avoiding conflicts of interest, promoters ensure that the company is established on a solid foundation. Failure to comply with these duties can lead to significant legal consequences, including the rescission of contracts, claims for damages, and the recovery of secret profits...

Nigeria's constitutional development has been a dynamic process, reflecting the country's diverse history and aspirations. From colonial constitutions to the current 1999 Constitution, each stage has contributed to shaping Nigeria's political and legal landscape. The ongoing amendments and reforms underscore the commitment to adapting the constitution to meet contemporary challenges and ensure the protection of citizens' rights and the stability of the federal republic...

Understanding the nuances of offences against persons, lawful and unlawful homicide, murder, and the principles of novus actus interveniens is crucial for the proper application of criminal law in Nigeria. These principles are embedded in the Criminal Code Act and have been interpreted and applied in numerous cases to ensure justice and accountability...

Promissory estoppel, laches, and acquiescence are equitable doctrines aimed at ensuring fairness and justice. They prevent parties from acting inconsistently with their previous conduct, causing harm to others who relied on such conduct. These principles are well-recognized in Nigerian jurisprudence and are essential tools for achieving equitable outcomes in various legal disputes...

The relationship of law to justice, morality, religion, social change, and ethics in Nigeria is complex and multifaceted. Laws are influenced by moral and religious values, drive social change, and are underpinned by ethical principles. Nigerian case law and statutes illustrate these relationships, highlighting the dynamic and evolving nature of the legal system...

Succession rights in Nigeria are multifaceted, involving statutory law, customary law, and Islamic law. Understanding the applicable legal framework is crucial for the proper distribution of a deceased person's estate. Statutes and case law provide guidance on the validity of wills, order of inheritance, and the administration of estates, ensuring that the deceased's wishes and the rights of heirs are respected...

The law of evidence in Nigeria is comprehensive and essential for the proper administration of justice. Understanding its concepts, rules, and applicability ensures that legal proceedings are conducted fairly and that the truth is effectively established. The Evidence Act, 2011 serves as the foundation for these principles and guides the courts in their deliberations on what constitutes admissible and credible evidence...

Legal research is a systematic process of identifying and retrieving information necessary to support legal decision-making. It involves the application of various methods to gather legal information and analyze legal issues. Effective legal research ensures that legal professionals have access to the most relevant and up-to-date information...

Conflicts between law and morals can arise, leading to debates on whether laws should be changed to reflect moral views or if moral views should be adapted to comply with existing laws. For instance, debates on the legality and morality of same-sex relationships continue to generate controversy in Nigeria...
